# User Guide

Cost Tracker is a self-hosted household expense-sharing app for partners who share living expenses.

Unlike event-based splitters (Splitwise) or budgeting tools (YNAB), Cost Tracker is designed for
the continuous rhythm of shared living: log expenses as they happen, review the balance together,
settle monthly via bank transfer, and repeat.

## Who Is It For?

- **Couples or housemates** sharing everyday expenses (groceries, utilities, dining out)
- **Self-hosters** who want full control over their financial data
- **Privacy-conscious users** who prefer not to send expense data to third-party services

## How It Works

The core loop is simple:

1. **Log expenses** as they happen — quick capture on mobile, batch entry on desktop
2. **Check the balance** to see who owes whom
3. **Settle monthly** — sit together, review unsettled expenses, confirm the total, and transfer
   via your bank
4. **Repeat** — the balance resets after each settlement

## Key Concepts

**Expenses** are shared costs paid by one person on behalf of the household. Each expense tracks
who paid and how the cost is split between partners.

**Split types** determine how an expense is divided:

- **Even** — split equally (the default)
- **Shares** — weighted split (e.g., 2:1 ratio)
- **Percentage** — percentage-based (e.g., 60/40)
- **Exact** — specific amounts per person

**Settlements** are formal records of "settling up." When you settle, selected expenses are locked
as paid and a record of who transferred what to whom is created. Settled expenses can no longer
be edited or deleted.

**Recurring costs** are templates for expenses that repeat on a schedule (rent, subscriptions,
insurance). They can auto-generate expenses when due or be triggered manually.
